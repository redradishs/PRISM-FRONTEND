Privacy Policy

Overview: This Privacy Policy explains what data PRISM collects, how it is used, and your rights under the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173).

1. Data We Collect

  • Account Information: name, school email, role (Student, Instructor, Admin).
  • Institutional Affiliation: program/department, coordinator status (if applicable).
  • Authentication Data: login timestamps, verification status, tokens (e.g., from Google sign-in).
  • Device & Technical Data: browser type/version, OS, device identifiers, IP address, error logs.

2. How We Use Your Data

  • Provide and operate PRISM services, including assessments and analytics.
  • Authenticate users and secure accounts; detect, prevent, and investigate misuse.
  • Personalize experience (e.g., role-based features) and improve platform performance.
  • Provide support, respond to requests, and send important service notices.
  • Comply with institutional policies and applicable laws.

3. Legal Basis & Compliance

  • Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173): We comply with all provisions of the Philippine Data Privacy Act.
  • Institutional necessity to deliver academic services.
  • Consent (e.g., Google sign-in) where required.
  • Legitimate interests, such as platform security and service improvement.

4. Third-Party Services

  • Authentication providers (e.g., Google) to facilitate secure sign-in.
  • AI (Meta Llama 3.1-8B-Instruct) for Assisted Generation and Recommendation of Resources.
  • Hosting and infrastructure providers to deliver the service.
  • These providers process data on our behalf under appropriate safeguards.

5. Data Retention

  • We retain data for as long as necessary to provide services and meet legal/academic obligations.
  • Backups and logs may persist for a limited period after account changes or deletion.

6. Data Security

  • We implement administrative, technical, and organizational measures to protect your data.
  • No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. We continuously improve our safeguards.

7. Your Rights Under RA 10173

Under the Data Privacy Act of 2012, you have the following rights:

  • Right to be Informed: Know how your data is collected, used, and shared.
  • Right to Access: Request copies of your personal data.
  • Right to Correction: Update or correct inaccurate information.
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal requirements).
  • Right to Object: Object to processing in certain situations.
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured format.

To exercise these rights, contact administrator@prismgcccs.live.

8. International Transfers

Data may be processed on servers outside your region. We use appropriate safeguards consistent with institutional requirements.

11. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Continued use of PRISM constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

12. Contact

For data requests or concerns, contact administrator@prismgcccs.live.
